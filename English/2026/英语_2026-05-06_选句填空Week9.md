## 📝 错题 #3

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：选句填空（Week 9 - The Great Migration）

### 【题目原文】
**Directions:** Read the following passage. Fill in each blank with a proper sentence given in the box. Each sentence can be used only once. Note that there are two more sentences than you need.

**选项：**
A. That was against the law for African Americans at the time.
B. But during World War I, migration from Europe almost stopped.
C. The South actually did everything it could to keep the people from leaving.
D. The people of the Great Migration met with tremendous resistance in the North.
E. So this ended up being nearly a complete redistribution of part of an entire people.
F. They finally chose what they would do with their talents and where they would pursue them.

**The Great Migration**

The Great Migration began when the North had a labor problem. The North had been relying on cheap labor from Europe—immigrants from Europe—to work the factories, the foundries and the steel mills. **(36)___** As a result, the North decided to go and find the cheapest labor in the land, which meant many of African Americans in the South were not even being paid for their hard work. Many were sharecroppers working for the right to live on the land that they were farming.

But it turned out that the South did not take kindly to this poaching of its cheap labor. **(37)___** They would arrest people from the railroad platforms and from their train seats. And when there were too many people to arrest, they would wave the train on through so that people who had been hoping and saving and praying for the chance to get to freedom had to figure out: How now will we get out?

Before the Great Migration began, 90 percent of all African Americans were living in the South, where they were nearly held captive. But by the time this Great Migration was over, nearly half were living all over the rest of the country. **(38)___**

This Great Migration was the first time in American history that the lowest caste people signaled that they had options for themselves and were willing to take them. **(39)___** Think about those cotton fields, rice plantations, tobacco fields and sugar plantations, where there were opera singers, jazz musicians, playwrights, novelists, surgeons, attorneys, accountants, professors, and journalists. And how do we know that? We know that because that is what they and their children and now their grandchildren and even great-grandchildren have often chosen to become once they had the chance to choose for themselves what they would do with their God-given talents.

### 【你的答案】
36. B | 37. A | 38. C | 39. F

### 【正确答案】
36. **B** ✅ | 37. **C** ❌ | 38. **E** ❌ | 39. **F** ✅ | **正确率：2/4**

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(37) 总述+举例关系判断失误**：空格后是具体行动（逮捕火车站上的人、放行火车）。**C**（南方尽其所能阻止人们离开）是**总述**，后文的逮捕、放行火车都是**举例**支持 C。A（这对非裔美国人来说是违法的）语义不够贴切——这段强调的是南方**实际做了什么**来阻止，而非法律是否合法。
2. **(38) 数据总结句遗漏**：前文是数据对比——从"90% 在南方"到"近一半遍布全国"，这是一个**人口重新分布**的结果。**E**（这最终导致了整个民族一部分人的近乎完全重新分布）完美总结了这个数据变化。C（南方阻止人们离开）这段讲的是南方阻挠，但 38 题的位置是在讲迁徙**完成后的结果**，时间线不对。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 总述+举例：C 是总述，逮捕/放行火车是举例
- 数据总结：E 是对 90%→近一半的数据总结
- 语义呼应：F 的 chosen 与文末 chosen to become 形成语义链条
- 段落基调：选句要符合段落整体情感倾向（39 题 D 与段落积极基调不符）

### 【解题思路】
1. **找总述+举例**：空格后是具体例子 → 空格是总述句（37 题）
2. **找数据总结**：前文有具体数据变化 → 空格是总结句（38 题）
3. **注意段落基调**：选句要符合段落整体情感倾向，39 题段落是积极肯定，D 是消极阻力，基调不符
4. **关键词复现**：F 的 chosen 与文末 chosen to become 形成呼应
5. **排除干扰项**：A 的"违法"文中无法律依据线索；D 的"阻力"与段落基调不符

### 💡 举一反三

1. **总述+举例结构**：The government took many measures to control pollution. **First**, factories were required to install filters. **Second**, emissions were strictly limited.
2. **数据总结句**：In 1900, only 10% of Americans had access to electricity. By 1950, that figure had risen to 90%. **This represented a dramatic transformation of American life.**
3. **段落基调匹配**：选句填空要注意段落整体情感倾向，积极段落不选消极句，消极段落不选积极句。
