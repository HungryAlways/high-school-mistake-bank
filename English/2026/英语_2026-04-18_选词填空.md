## 选词填空错题整理（第 55-61 题）

### 【完整原文】
> People Think Meals Taste Better If They Are Expensive
>
> It is said that there's no such thing as a free lunch, but even if you manage to bag a bargain meal, it will not taste as good as a more expensive meal, according to scientists.
>
> A new study has found that restaurant (36) ______ who pay more for their meals think the food is tastier than if it is offered for a smaller price. The experts think that people tend to associate cost with quality and this changes their (37) ______ of how food tastes.
>
> Scientists at Cornell University in New York studied the eating habits of 139 people enjoying an Italian buffet (自助餐) in a restaurant. The price of the food was set by the (38) ______ at either $4 or $8 for the all-you-can-eat meal. Customers were asked to (39) ______ how good the food tasted, the quality of the restaurant and to leave their names.
>
> The experiment (40) ______ that the people who paid $8 for the food enjoyed their meal 11 percent more than those who ate the"cheaper" buffet. Interestingly those that paid for the $4 buffet said they felt guiltier about loading up their plates and felt that they (41) ______. However, the scientists said that both groups ate around the same quantity of food in total, according to the study (42) ______ at the Experimental Biology meeting this week.
>
> Brian Wansink, a professor of (43) ______ behaviour at the university, said:"We were fascinated to find that pricing has little impact on how much one eats, but a huge impact on how you (44) ______ the experience." He thinks that people enjoyed their food more as they associated cost with quality and that small changes to a restaurant can change how tasty people find their meals.
>
> In a(n) (45) ______ study, scientists from the university showed that people who eat in dim lighting consume 175 less calories (卡路里) than people who eat in brightly lit areas.
>
> **单词选项列表**：A. overate | B. precious | C. rate | D. researchers | AB. impression | AC. previous | AD. presented | BC. interpret | BD. goers | CD. revealed | ABC. consumer

---

### 第 55 题（第 36 题）：restaurant goers（餐厅顾客）
- **题目**：`A new study has found that restaurant (36) ______ who pay more for their meals...`
- ❌ 错误答案：**ABC (consumer)**
- ✅ 正确答案：**BD (goers)**
- **解析**：`restaurant goers` = **餐厅顾客**，`goer` 指"常去某地的人"。`restaurant goers` 是更地道的固定表达，指"光顾餐厅的人"。
- **💡 记忆**：`goer` = 去某处的人（如 `moviegoer` 电影观众）

### 第 56 题（第 38 题）：researchers（研究者）
- **题目**：`The price of the food was set by the (38) ______ at either $4 or $8...`
- ❌ 错误答案：**BD (goers)**
- ✅ 正确答案：**D (researchers)**
- **解析**：价格是由**研究者**设定的，而非顾客。`researchers` = 研究者、科学家。`set by the researchers` = 由研究者设定。
- **💡 解题**：看主语动作——"设定价格"是研究者的职责，不是顾客的行为

### 第 57 题（第 39 题）：rate（评估/打分）
- **题目**：`Customers were asked to (39) ______ how good the food tasted...`
- ❌ 错误答案：**BC (interpret)**
- ✅ 正确答案：**C (rate)**
- **解析**：`rate` = **评估、打分**。`rate how good the food tasted` = 评估食物有多好吃。`interpret`（解释/理解）语义不符——顾客不是"解释"食物有多好吃，而是"评价/打分"。
- **💡 记忆**：`rate` = 评估、评级（如 `rate the service` 评价服务）

### 第 58 题（第 40 题）：revealed（揭示/显示）
- **题目**：`The experiment (40) ______ that the people who paid $8 for the food enjoyed their meal 11 percent more...`
- ❌ 错误答案：**AD (presented)**
- ✅ 正确答案：**CD (revealed)**
- **解析**：`revealed that...` = **揭示/显示**（某事实），常用于实验、研究结果。`presented that` 搭配不当，`present` 通常接 `present + 名词` 或 `present + to + sb`，不接 `that` 从句。
- **💡 记忆**：实验/研究显示 → `reveal / show / demonstrate that...`

### 第 59 题（第 42 题）：presented（提出/展示）
- **题目**：`...according to the study (42) ______ at the Experimental Biology meeting this week.`
- ❌ 错误答案：**D (researchers)**
- ✅ 正确答案：**AD (presented)**
- **解析**：`the study presented at...` = **在……会议上提出的研究**。过去分词作后置定语，`study` 与 `present` 是被动关系（研究被提出）。
- **💡 记忆**：`present a study at a meeting` = 在会议上提出/展示一项研究

### 第 60 题（第 43 题）：consumer（消费者）
- **题目**：`Brian Wansink, a professor of (43) ______ behaviour at the university...`
- ❌ 错误答案：**CD (revealed)**
- ✅ 正确答案：**ABC (consumer)**
- **解析**：`consumer behaviour` = **消费者行为学**，是学科名称。`consumer` 修饰 `behaviour`，表示"消费者的行为"。`revealed behaviour` 语义不通。
- **💡 记忆**：`consumer behavior` = 消费者行为学（心理学/经济学分支学科）

### 第 61 题（第 44 题）：interpret（解读/看待）
- **题目**：`...but a huge impact on how you (44) ______ the experience.`
- ❌ 错误答案：**B (precious)**
- ✅ 正确答案：**BC (interpret)**
- **解析**：`interpret the experience` = **如何看待/解读这段体验**。`interpret` = 解读、理解、看待。`precious`（珍贵的）是形容词，不能作谓语动词。
- **💡 记忆**：`interpret` = 解读、理解（`how you interpret something` = 你如何看待某事）
