# Unit 1 No Limits Exercise 6 语法篇章填空 Animals Sacrificed in the Name of Fashion

## 【完整原文】

Every year, billions of animals suffer and die for clothing and accessories. Skin __1__ (tear) from them to make leather. Birds are held down while handfuls of feathers are ripped out of their sensitive skin. Small animals are caged for life __2__ being killed for their fur. Sheep are often beaten and mutilated by workers stealing their wool. Goats are similarly abused for cashmere. All this is done in the name of fashion. __3__ type of material or where it comes from, if the process involves an animal, it will involve horrific cruelty.

Humans have used leather, wool and fur for years, and billions of animals are brutally slaughtered each year for the clothing industry. Mass production of fur and leather items has created a cruel system of farming, trapping, and skinning, with uproar __4__ (spark) among animal rights activists.

The vast majority of the billions of animals __5__ are killed for their skin, wool, feathers, or fur have to endure all the horrors of factory farming. Sensitive living beings are forced to live in narrow and dirty conditions, and every bit of freedom and autonomy is taken away from them. Animals suffer at the hands of workers, who __6__ roughly handle, kick, and beat them. After a lifetime of torment (痛苦), some are violently killed on a large scale __7__ the cheapest means possible, including bludgeoning and electrocution, and __8__ are even slaughtered (屠宰) and skinned alive. What's more, trapped animals used for their fur can suffer for days from blood loss, shock, dehydration, frostbite, and gangrene or be attacked by predators before trappers return to kill them.

We're the consumer who need to know and understand the incredible amount of suffering and devastation __9__ (present) in each piece of fur. Let's start to shed some more light on how animals are killed in the name of fashion! This cruelty and the fur trade in general are certain to come to an end only __10__ consumers refuse to buy or wear animal fur of any kind.

> 注：第 3 空为三词短语，按整卷计为 1 个空（No matter what）。

## 【逐题错误分析】

### 第 1 题
- **is teared → is torn**：tear（撕/剥）是不规则动词，过去分词是 **torn**，不是 teared（teared 是 tear "流泪"义的规则形式，此处词义不对）。皮肤是"被从动物身上剥下"，用被动 is torn（from them）。
- 三态：tear - tore - torn。

### 第 2 题
- **which → before**：这里 "caged for life ___ being killed"（被关一辈子，直到被杀）表时间先后，用介词 **before** + doing。which 是关系代词，此处无先行词也无从句结构，不能用。

### 第 4 题
- **sparted → sparked**：拼写错误，动词是 **spark**（引发），过去分词 sparked。with uproar sparked among... 是 "with + 宾语 + 过去分词" 的独立主格结构（抗议被引发）。
- 注意没有 sparted 这个词。

### 第 5 题
- **who → which**：先行词是 the billions of animals（动物），指物用 **which**（或 that），不用 who（who 指人）。which 在从句中作主语（which are killed for their skin）。

### 第 6 题
- **many → may**：空后是动词原形 roughly handle，需要情态动词。**may**（可能会）表推测，who may roughly handle...（那些可能会粗暴对待动物的工人）。many 是形容词/代词，不能接动词原形。

### 第 7 题
- **where → by**："killed ... ___ the cheapest means possible"（用最廉价的方式杀死）。**by + means**（通过……方式/手段）是固定搭配。where 引导地点，此处不是地点关系。
- 记：by this means / by means of（凭借……手段）。

### 第 8 题
- **another → others**：前文 "some are violently killed..."，此处 "___ are even slaughtered..." 与 some 呼应，构成 **some... others...**（一些……另一些……）。others 泛指"另外一些"（复数，谓语 are）；another 指"再一个"（单数），与复数谓语 are 不搭。

### 第 9 题
- **presenting → presented**：suffering and devastation（苦难与破坏）与 present 是被动关系——苦难是"被呈现/存在于"每件皮草中。过去分词 **presented** 作后置定语（= that is presented in each piece）。presenting 是主动，语义不对。

## 【知识点】

1. **不规则动词过去分词**：tear-tore-**torn**（撕/剥）；辨析 tear 的两个义项（撕 vs 流泪）拼写不同。
2. **被动 vs 主动的分词定语**：devastation **presented**（被呈现，过去分词）；skin **is torn**（被剥下，被动语态）。逻辑主语是承受者→用被动/过去分词。
3. **关系代词的选择**：先行词指物用 **which/that**，指人用 who。第5题 animals → which。
4. **情态动词 + 动词原形**：who **may** roughly handle（may 表推测，后接原形）。
5. **介词固定搭配**：
   - **before** doing（在……之前，第2题）
   - **by** the cheapest means（通过……方式，第7题）
6. **不定代词呼应**：**some... others...**（一些……另一些……，第8题）；another（再一个，单数）不能配复数谓语。
7. **让步状语**：**No matter what**（无论……，第3题，✓）= whatever。
8. **only when + 从句**：only when consumers refuse...（只有当……时，第10题，✓）。

## 【举一反三】

1. The old book was so worn that several pages had been __ (tear) out.
   参考：**torn**（被撕掉，过去分词，tear-tore-torn）
2. There were many candidates; some passed the interview, while __ failed.
   参考：**others**（some... others... 呼应，复数）
3. The problem __ (discuss) at the meeting yesterday has been solved.
   参考：**discussed**（问题是被讨论的，过去分词作后置定语）
