## 📝 错题 #1

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：语法填空

### 【题目原文】
**Week 11 — Grammar**

Directions: After reading the passage below, fill in the blanks to make the passage coherent and grammatically correct. For the blanks with a given word, fill in each blank with the proper form of the given word; for the other blanks, use one word that best fits each blank.

**Raleigh International**

Four students from National University have recently returned from Africa, where they participated in development projects with local residents in rural areas. For 10 weeks, these young people worked in Zimbabwe and Botswana as members of a program called Raleigh International.

Raleigh International, which (1) ______ (found) in 1984, gives young people a chance to do volunteer work while experiencing other countries and their cultures. The program, (2) ______ was named after the explorer Sir Walter Raleigh, encourages young people to help others and work in difficult settings. More than 30,000 students from a variety of cultures and backgrounds have volunteered in a total of 40 countries.

Marisa Silvestri, 20, a business major, traveled to Zimbabwe to work with a group in the Mavuradonha Wilderness Area. Their first project was helping farmers build electric fences to protect their crops from (3) ______ (destroy) by elephants. When elephants entered the village one night, the students joined with farmers (4) ______ (drive) them away and preserve the fields. Silvestri's group also constructed small cottages for tourists to stay in, (5) ______ (build) in the local style.

Saeed Mohammed, also 20 and an engineering major, joined (6) ______ group in Zimbabwe. His group carried out a survey of health problems in several villages and taught children about good health habits, (7) ______ impressed Saeed was that the kids were so curious (8) ______ the Raleigh International students that after the health lessons, they asked them questions about "everything else under the sun".

Saeed's group also worked to build foot trails through the forest. While they were out working in the bush, a fire (9) ______ (break) out, destroying eight kilometers of forest. The students fought to bring the fire under control, sleeping only 45 minutes during the two days.

In Botswana, two 19-year-old students, Lakeshia Young and David Min, helped to build a clinic and a meeting hall in a small village. (10) ______ they didn't face angry elephants, they said the building work was much harder than they expected. "It was a great learning experience," Young said.

### 【你的答案】
(1) founded | (2) which | (3) being destroyed | (4) to drive | (5) built | (6) a | (7) What | (8) that | (9) breaking | (10) Although

### 【正确答案】
(1) was founded | (2) which | (3) being destroyed | (4) to drive | (5) built | (6) another | (7) What | (8) about | (9) broke | (10) Although

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(1) founded → was founded**：定语从句中 which 作主语，需要完整的谓语动词。found 表示"建立"，Raleigh International 是被建立的，且发生在 1984 年（过去），应使用一般过去时的被动语态 **was founded**。
2. **(6) a → another**：前文已提到 Marisa 在 Zimbabwe 加入了一个小组，Saeed 加入的是**另一个**小组，用 another 体现上下文递进关系。
3. **(8) that → about**：**curious about sb/sth** 是固定搭配，意为"对某人/某事好奇"。句中还包含 so…that… 句型（如此……以至于……），两个 that 不是同一成分，容易混淆。
4. **(9) breaking → broke**：a fire 是主语，需要谓语动词。全文叙述过去的事情，用一般过去时 **broke**。逗号后的 destroying 是现在分词作结果状语，一个简单句只能有一个谓语。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 定语从句中的被动语态（which + was/were + 过去分词）
- 不定代词 another 的用法（表"又一个，另一个"）
- 固定搭配 curious about
- so…that… 句型
- 谓语动词与分词作状语的区别
- 一般过去时的被动语态

### 【解题思路】
1. **判断句子成分**：先确定空格所在句子的主语、谓语是否完整，判断是否需要谓语动词。
2. **注意上下文逻辑**：another 与前文 a group 形成递进，需要联系上下文判断。
3. **记住固定搭配**：curious about 是高频搭配，看到 curious 先想 about。
4. **区分谓语和非谓语**：一个简单句只能有一个谓语动词，逗号后面如果已有分词（如 destroying），前面的空格就是谓语。

## 📝 错题 #2

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：选词填空

### 【题目原文】
**Week 11 — Vocabulary**

Directions: Complete the following passage by using the words in the box. Each word can only be used once. Note that there is one word more than you need.

A. attention | B. collections | C. considerably | D. delicacy | E. engaged
F. essence | G. genius | H. incomparable | I. probably | J. remarkable | K. reproduced

---

**Mozart Portrait Miniature of "Virtually Unparalleled"**

With his pink cheeks and bright eyes Mozart looks positively innocent, the very picture of a young man in love.

The image is contained in an incredibly rare portrait miniature (袖珍画像), (11) ______ in colour for what is thought to be the first time and about to be sold at auction after centuries of being passed down through generations of the same family.

It's announced that the family was to sell one of only two Mozart portraits which remain in private hands, with the auction house's head of books and manuscripts describing the object's significance as "virtually (12) ______."

He said the artist had caught the very (13) ______ of Mozart. "He looked directly out at you, whereas there are lots of portrait miniatures where their eyes might as well be closed. This was different. When I looked at it for the first time, it seemed he (14) ______ you immediately. It was very open and friendly. You were looking at a(n) (15) ______ as a real person."

The 21-year-old composer gave the portrait to his 18-year-old cousin Maria Anna Thekla Mozart, (16) ______ his very first lover.

The portrait, given as a love token, clearly meant a lot to Maria Anna, given that it was found among her belongings when she died in 1841, aged 82. "She kept it for 70-odd years, which is touching," said Roe.

Mozart meanwhile soon turned his (17) ______ to the soprano Aloysia Weber from Mannheim, with whom he was deeply head over heels in love.

There are 12 known portraits of Mozart, 10 of which are in public (18) ______. This 4 cm portrait by an unknown artist has been made on ivory and has its original bezel (镶嵌面板) and glass. It is not top end, but is a thing of beauty, Roe said, pointing to the (19) ______ detail in the face "formed by the tiniest dots of gouache (水粉) applied with the utmost precision and (20) ______."

The piece is expected to fetch between £200,000 and £300,000.

### 【你的答案】
(11) E | (12) G | (13) J | (14) K | (15) F | (16) I | (17) B | (18) A | (19) H | (20) D

### 【正确答案】
(11) K.reproduced | (12) H.incomparable | (13) F.essence | (14) E.engaged | (15) G.genius | (16) I.probably | (17) A.attention | (18) B.collections | (19) J.remarkable | (20) D.delicacy

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(11) engaged → reproduced**：reproduce in colour = 以彩色翻印/复制。过去分词作后置定语，修饰 portrait miniature。
2. **(12) genius → incomparable**：incomparable（无与伦比的）与标题 "Virtually Unparalleled"（几乎无与伦比）**同义呼应**，描述这幅画的重要性/价值。
3. **(13) remarkable → essence**：**catch the very essence of…** = 抓住……的本质/神韵。essence 是名词，remarkable 是形容词，词性不符。
4. **(14) reproduced → engaged**：**engage sb** = 吸引某人。画像的眼睛直视你，仿佛立刻吸引了你。
5. **(15) essence → genius**：genius = 天才。你在看着一个有血有肉的天才本人。essence 已用在 13。
6. **(17) collections → attention**：**turn one's attention to…** = 把注意力转向……莫扎特不久后把注意力转向了女高音 Aloysia Weber。
7. **(18) attention → collections**：**public collections** = 公共收藏（博物馆、美术馆等）。
8. **(19) incomparable → remarkable**：remarkable detail = 非凡的细节。incomparable 已用在 12。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 固定搭配：catch the essence of… / turn one's attention to… / public collections
- 同义词呼应：unparalleled ≈ incomparable
- 词性判断：名词/动词/形容词/副词
- 过去分词作后置定语：reproduced in colour
- 动词搭配：engage sb（吸引某人）

### 【解题思路】
1. **先标词性**：把选项按词性分类，先确定每个空需要什么词性。
2. **找固定搭配**：catch the essence of / turn one's attention to / public collections。
3. **注意上下文呼应**：标题 "Unparalleled" 和 incomparable 是同义词呼应。
4. **排除法**：用掉的词划掉，避免重复。
5. **注意词形**：选项给出的可能是原形，需要根据语法判断是否需要变形（本题选项可直接填入）。

## 📝 错题 #3

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：完形填空

### 【题目原文】
**Week 11 — Cloze**

Directions: For each blank in the following passage there are four words or phrases marked A, B, C and D. Fill in each blank with the word or phrase that best fits the context.

---

"Every technology has improved over the years except batteries! Why can't someone invent a better battery?" These are the words we often hear from technology fans. In fact, though, the average technology fan is **(21) ______** two huge points about batteries.

First point: The batteries you probably think about most are the ones in your phone or laptop. But you could argue—and many scientists do—that batteries are the **(22) ______** to handling much, much bigger problems, like energy, transportation and climate change.

**(23) ______**, today electric cars represent only about 1 percent of U.S. new car sales. One reason is that they cost more than gas-powered cars. Another is range **(24) ______**—consumers' fear that they'll run out of charge far from home. The **(25) ______** higher-capacity batteries now under development aim to solve both of those problems.

Then there's the grid (输电网). Electric utilities spend their days coping with swings in energy **(26) ______**. There's almost no demand at night, when everyone's asleep, and then tremendous increase at 5 p.m., when people get home from work. Utilities actually **(27) ______** expensive, inefficient, occasionally used backup power plants just to handle demand increase, as occurs during heat waves.

Batteries connected to the grid could **(28) ______** those strange swings. Maybe even more important, grid batteries could capture solar power while the sun's shining—and wind power when it's blowing—for use when we really need it. Thus far we haven't been able to make the sun and wind respect our lifestyle **(29) ______**.

The other important point: Batteries have been getting better over the decades. The reason why we don't **(30) ______** it is that our devices have been getting faster, more powerful and more power-hungry at the same time. If you could put a **(31) ______** iPhone battery into a 1995 phone, it'd probably go a year on a single charge.

Other great things are **(32) ______**. Materials scientist Mike Zimmerman has succeeded in replacing the liquid electrolyte (液体电解质), which can burn easily, with a single piece of special plastic film. That is, batteries are **(33) ______** exploding. And because it's unblowuppable, Zimmerman can use lithium metal, which has a much higher energy density but is considered too **(34) ______** to use with today's liquid-electrolyte batteries.

So if you do want to complain about your batteries, get it out now. It won't be long before they have a much better **(35) ______**. Let's wait and see!

---

**选项**：
21. A. criticizing B. following C. missing D. weighing
22. A. keys B. attitudes C. resistances D. reactions
23. A. In conclusion B. In addition C. By contrast D. For example
24. A. anxiety B. requirement C. function D. burden
25. A. stronger B. cheaper C. safer D. cleaner
26. A. price B. demand C. stock D. control
27. A. maintain B. evaluate C. abandon D. identify
28. A. find out B. hand out C. pick out D. even out
29. A. incidents B. directions C. schedules D. purposes
30. A. notice B. doubt C. support D. ignore
31. A. charged B. used C. dead D. modern
32. A. in order B. in the darkness C. on the way D. under control
33. A. inconvenient for B. incapable of C. sensitive to D. different from
34. A. innovative B. risky C. controversial D. economical
35. A. investigation B. opportunity C. investment D. reputation

### 【你的答案】
21. D | 22. C | 23. A | 24. A | 25. C | 26. A | 27. C | 28. C | 29. B | 30. A | 31. D | 32. C | 33. D | 34. B | 35. C

### 【正确答案】
21. C.missing | 22. A.keys | 23. D.For example | 24. A.anxiety | 25. B.cheaper | 26. B.demand | 27. A.maintain | 28. D.even out | 29. C.schedules | 30. A.notice | 31. D.modern | 32. C.on the way | 33. B.incapable of | 34. B.risky | 35. B.opportunity

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(21) weighing → missing**：missing = 忽略、没注意到。人们抱怨电池没进步，但实际上**忽略了**两个重要事实。weighing = 权衡，语义不符。
2. **(22) resistances → keys**：**keys to** = …的关键。电池是解决能源、交通等大问题的**关键**。resistances = 阻力，语义完全相反。
3. **(23) In conclusion → For example**：电动车是前文"能源、交通、气候变化"这些大问题的**具体例子**，用 For example。In conclusion（总之）用于总结，这里不是总结。
4. **(25) safer → cheaper**：前文提到两个问题：电动车**成本更高**（cost more）和**里程焦虑**。更高容量 + 规模效应 → 电池**更便宜**，从而解决价格问题。
5. **(26) price → demand**：后文解释：晚上几乎没需求，下午 5 点大幅增加——这是能源**需求**的波动。energy demand = 能源需求，固定搭配。
6. **(27) abandon → maintain**：**maintain** = 维持、运营。公用事业公司需要**维持**这些备用电厂以应对需求高峰。
7. **(28) pick out → even out**：**even out** = 使平稳、消除波动。电网电池可以**平抑**能源需求的波动。
8. **(29) directions → schedules**：**schedules** = 时间表。太阳不会因为我们下班才出来，风也不会因为我们需要才吹。
9. **(33) sensitive to → incapable of**：用特殊塑料薄膜代替易燃液体电解质 → 电池**无法**爆炸了。**incapable of** = 不能……的。
10. **(35) investment → opportunity**：电池很快会有更好的**前景/机会**。结合上下文，电池技术即将迎来突破。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 词义辨析：missing / weighing / maintain / abandon
- 固定搭配：keys to / range anxiety / energy demand / even out / incapable of / on the way
- 逻辑连接词：For example（举例）vs In conclusion（总结）
- 上下文推理：前文 cost more → 后文 cheaper 解决价格问题
- 短语辨析：even out（使平稳）/ find out（找出）/ pick out（挑选）/ hand out（分发）

### 【解题思路】
1. **通读全文**：先了解文章主题（电池技术被误解），把握整体逻辑。
2. **判断逻辑关系**：举例（For example）还是总结（In conclusion）？
3. **找固定搭配**：range anxiety / energy demand / even out / incapable of。
4. **上下文呼应**：前文提到 cost more → 后文 cheaper 解决价格问题。
5. **排除干扰项**：有些选项单个看似乎合理，但需要结合全文逻辑判断。
6. **注意词义细微差别**：maintain（维持运营）vs abandon（放弃）vs identify（识别）。
