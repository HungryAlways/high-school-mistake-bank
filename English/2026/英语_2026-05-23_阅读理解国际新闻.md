## 📝 错题 #4

**学科**:英语 | **章节**:阅读理解(新闻类/综合信息)
**知识点**:细节理解与推理判断, 上下文词义猜测, 文章出处与体裁判断, 长难句与修辞理解, 新闻类阅读策略

### 【题目原文】
(B)

◆ Landerneau, France
Tour de France crash: A Tour de France spectator was arrested this week for causing a mass crash that injured nine cyclists during the event's first stage. The 30-year-old French woman, whose name has not been released, stepped in with a sign reading "Go, Grandpa and Grandma!" and clipped German rider Tony Martin, who lost his balance, fell, and set off a series of collisions. It looked like a war zone. German athlete Jasha Sutterlin dropped out of the competition because of injuries suffered during the crash. The woman is accused of involuntarily causing injury and putting the lives of others at risk; she could face up to two years in prison and a fine of $35,000.

◆ London, Britain
Health minister disgraced: Battling criticism that he mishandled Britain's pandemic response, Health Minister Matt Hancock was forced to resign this week after being caught on video kissing and embracing a female assistant, Gina Coladango, in violation of the pandemic distancing rules. Prime Minister Boris Johnson initially stood by Hancock, but after the Health Minister was criticized by the press and fellow Conservative lawmakers, Johnson accepted his resignation. Hancock had frequently lectured the public to stay 6 feet away from non-family members, and once described lockdown-breaking actions as "a matter for the police".

◆ Kampala, Uganda
Delta surges across Africa: Ugandan President Yoweri Museveni called on citizens to plead for divine intervention (上帝之佑) this week as the Delta variant of the pandemic spread out through his country, where the hospitals were completely overwhelmed and the medical facilities ran out of oxygen. Uganda is in full lockdown, with only vehicles carrying cargo or the sick allowed on the roads and employees forced to sleep at their workplaces. Many other African countries are in a similar plight. It's reported that in just the past week, pandemic infections on the continent had jumped by 31 percent to 5.4 million, and deaths by 19 percent, to 142,000.

25. What can you learn from the passage?
A. The Tour de France became the frontline of a war zone because of a 30-year-old French woman.

26. What does the underlined word "plight" mean according to the passage?

27. In which section would this passage most probably be found in the Newsweek magazine?

### 【选项】
- A. The Tour de France became the frontline of a war zone because of a 30-year-old French woman.
- B. The British Prime Minister resigned his post because he hugged and kissed a female assistant.
- C. A growing number of African people had been infected with the pandemic in the past week due to the Delta variant.
- D. Jasha Sutterlin no longer participated in the competition for fear of being infected with the pandemic.
- A. a difficult situation
- B. an easily-broken promise
- C. a state of being unfair
- D. economic improvement
- A. Health Care
- B. Science and Technology
- C. Global News
- D. Editorial

### 【你的答案】
**25. C  26. A  27. C**（全对，准确率 100%）

### 【错误原因分析】
学生全对，无需记录错误。以下为常见易错点，供复习参考：
1. 第25题易错选A：易对比喻"like a war zone"字面理解；易选B：张冠李戴，辞职的是卫生部长非首相；易选D：受"pandemic"干扰，忽略Sutterlin是因车祸受伤退出。
2. 第26题易错点：脱离上下文猜词。需利用"similar plight"前后文（医院瘫痪、封锁、感染激增）推理出"困境"。
3. 第27题易错选A：以偏概全，只关注第三段疫情，忽略全文是国际综合新闻。

### 【解题思路】
【第25题解析】细节理解与推理判断题。
A项错误:原文第一段提到"It looked like a war zone."(现场看起来像战区一样),这是比喻手法,形容车祸现场混乱惨烈,并非真正变成战争前线,属于字面理解错误/偷换概念。
B项错误:原文第二段明确指出是"Health Minister Matt Hancock was forced to resign"(卫生部长汉考克被迫辞职),而非英国首相鲍里斯·约翰逊辞职,属于张冠李戴。
C项正确:原文第三段提到"It's reported that in just the past week, pandemic infections on the continent had jumped by 31 percent to 5.4 million...",且前文指出Delta变异株正在非洲蔓延,与选项表述完全一致。
D项错误:原文第一段末尾说明Jasha Sutterlin是"dropped out of the competition because of injuries suffered during the crash"(因车祸受伤退出比赛),而非"fear of being infected"(害怕感染),属于曲解文意。
综上,选C。

【第26题解析】词义猜测题。
根据上下文语境,第三段描述了乌干达及非洲多国面临Delta变异株肆虐、医院不堪重负、氧气耗尽、全国封锁等严峻状况。划线词"plight"所在句"Many other African countries are in a similar plight."意为"许多其他非洲国家也处于类似的______中"。结合前文的灾难性描述,可推断"plight"指"困境、危急情况"。A项"a difficult situation"(困难局面)符合语境。B项"轻易违背的承诺"、C项"不公平的状态"、D项"经济改善"均不符合上下文逻辑。故选A。

【第27题解析】文章出处/体裁判断题。
通读全文可知,文章由三个独立的新闻短讯组成,分别报道了法国环法自行车赛事故、英国卫生部长因违反防疫规定辞职、非洲多国Delta变异株疫情爆发及封锁措施。内容涵盖体育、政治、公共卫生等多个领域,且均为国际新闻事件。因此,该文章最可能出现在《新闻周刊》的"Global News(国际新闻)"版块。A项"Health Care"仅涵盖第三部分,片面;B项"Science and Technology"与内容无关;D项"Editorial(社论)"通常为表达观点评论的文章,而本文是客观新闻报道。故选C。
