## 📝 错题 #1

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：词汇填空（Week 9）

### 【题目原文】
**Directions:** Complete the following passage by using the words in the box. Each word can only be used once. Note that there is one word more than you need.

**选项：** A. current | B. forks | C. fool | D. provides | E. conception | F. creatively | G. response | H. literally | I. moral | J. ignorant | K. switching

**The Big Decisions**

Let's say you had the chance to become a vampire. With one magical bite you would gain immortality and superhuman strength. Your friends who have undergone the transformation tell you the experience is incredible, saying everything about their new existence **(11)___** them with fun, companionship and meaning.

Would you do it, even knowing that once changed you could never go back?

The difficulty of the choice is that you'd have to use your human self and preferences to try to guess whether you'd enjoy becoming a vampire. You would **(12)___** become a different self. How can you possibly know what it would feel like to be this different version of you or whether you would like it?

In her book Transformative Experience, L. A. Paul, a philosophy professor at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, says life is filled with decisions that are a bit like this. Life is filled with **(13)___** in the road in which you end up changing who you are and what you want.

People who have a child suddenly become different. Joining the military is another transformational experience. So are marrying, changing careers, immigrating, **(14)___** beliefs. In each of these cases the **(15)___** you is trying to make an important decision, without having the chance to know what it will feel like to be the future you.

Paul's point is that we're fundamentally **(16)___** about many of the biggest choices of our lives and that it's not possible to make purely rational decisions. "You shouldn't **(17)___** yourself," she writes. "You have no idea what you are getting into."

So how do you make transformational decisions? You have to ask the right questions, Paul argues. Ask, "Do I have a profound desire to discover what it would be like to be this new me, to experience this new mode of living?" As she puts it, "The best **(18)___** to this situation is to choose based on whether we want to discover who we'll become."

Personally, I think Paul's solution is incomplete. I'd say to really make these decisions well you need to step outside the modern **(19)___** of ourselves as cognitive creatures. After all, we're **(20)___** creatures. These days we think of a lot of decisions as if they were shopping choices. When we're shopping for something, we act as autonomous creatures who are looking for the product that will produce the most pleasure or utility. But choosing to have a child or selecting a spouse, faith or life course is not like that. It's probably safer to ask "What do I admire?" than "What do I want?"

### 【你的答案】
(11) H. literally | (12) F. creatively | (13) B. forks | (14) D. provides | (15) A. current | (16) C. fool | (17) J. ignorant | (18) G. response | (19) K. switching | (20) I. moral

### 【正确答案】
(11) **D. provides** | (12) **H. literally** | (13) **B. forks** | (14) **K. switching** | (15) **A. current** | (16) **J. ignorant** | (17) **C. fool** | (18) **G. response** | (19) **E. conception** | (20) **I. moral**

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(11) 词性判断失误**：看到 `___ sb. with sth.` 没有识别出 `provide sb. with sth.` 固定搭配，误选了副词 literally。应优先识别固定搭配。
2. **(12) 语义理解偏差**：creatively（有创意地）修饰 become 语义不通，literally 表示"真正地"，强调转变的真实性。
3. **(14) 平行结构遗漏**：前面全是动名词并列（marrying, changing, immigrating），后面也必须是动名词 switching，而非动词 provides。
4. **(16) 和 (17) 词性混淆**：ignorant（形容词）和 fool（动词）互换了。be ignorant about（不了解）vs. fool yourself（自欺欺人），注意词性匹配。
5. **(19) 搭配不熟悉**：the modern conception of ourselves（现代人对自我的认知），conception 表示"观念、概念"。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 固定搭配：provide sb. with sth. / be ignorant about / fool oneself / forks in the road
- 平行结构：动名词并列（marrying, changing, immigrating, switching）
- 词性判断：形容词（ignorant, current, moral）vs. 动词（provides, fool, switching）vs. 副词（literally）
- 抽象概念搭配：modern conception of ourselves

### 【解题思路】
1. **先判断词性**：根据空格前后结构判断需要名词、动词、形容词还是副词
2. **寻找固定搭配**：如 `___ sb. with sth.` → provides；`be ___ about` → ignorant
3. **注意平行结构**：看到前面是 -ing 形式，后面也必须是 -ing
4. **语义验证**：代入后检查是否符合上下文逻辑
5. **排除法**：用掉一个选项划掉一个，最后多余一个

### 💡 举一反三

1. **provide 搭配**：The school provides students **with** free meals. / Free meals are provided **for** students.
2. **ignorant 搭配**：He was **ignorant of** the dangers involved. = He was **ignorant about** the dangers.
3. **fork in the road**：We stand at a **fork in the road** — we must choose between two different paths.
4. **fool oneself**：Don't **fool yourself** into thinking it'll be easy.

## 📝 错题 #2

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：完形填空（Week 9 - 女性在压力下的决策）

### 【题目原文】
**Directions:** For each blank in the following passage there are four words or phrases marked A, B, C and D. Fill in each blank with the word or phrase that best fits the context.

Recently, Senator Kirsten Gillibrand of New York said that if we want to fix the gridlock in Congress, we need more women. Women are more focused on finding common ground and **(21)___**, she argued. Besides, neuroscientists have uncovered evidence suggesting that, when the **(22)___** is on, women bring unique strengths to decision making.

Mara Mather and Nichole R. Lighthall, two cognitive neuroscientists, are two of the many researchers who have found that under normal circumstances, when everything is low-key and **(23)___**, men and women make decisions about risk in similar ways. We gather the best information we can, we **(24)___** potential costs against potential gains, and then we choose how to act. **(25)___**, add stress to the situation and men and women begin to part ways.

Dr. Mather and her team taught people a simple computer gambling game, in which they got points for inflating digital balloons. The more they inflated each balloon, the greater its value, and the **(26)___** of popping it. When they were relaxed, men and women took similar risks and averaged a similar number of pumps. But after experiencing the cold water, the stressed women **(27)___** sooner, cashing out their winnings and going with the more guaranteed win. Stressed men did just the **(28)___**. They kept pumping—in one study averaging about 50 percent more pumps than the women—and risking more.

Of course, just because it works this way in a(n) **(29)___** doesn't mean the same thing happens in the messy real world. Do organizations with women **(30)___** actually make less risky and more empathetic decisions in stressful circumstances?

Some evidence suggests they do. Credit Suisse examined almost 2,400 global corporations from 2005 to 2011—including the years directly preceding and **(31)___** the financial crisis—and found that companies with at least one woman on their boards **(32)___** comparable companies with all-male boards by 26 percent.

**(33)___**, what often happens is that women are asked to lead only during periods of intense stress. It's called the glass cliff, in which highly qualified women are asked to lead organizations only in times of **(34)___**. Think of Mary T. Barra at General Motors and Marissa Mayer at Yahoo, who were both brought in only after things had begun to fall apart. If more women were key decision makers, perhaps organizations could respond effectively to small stresses, **(35)___** letting them escalate into huge ones. There's a good reason that we'd benefit from more women in positions of power.

**选项：**
21. A. socializing B. memorizing C. cooperating D. deciding
22. A. presentation B. pressure C. administration D. measure
23. A. understandable B. acceptable C. manageable D. remarkable
24. A. fight B. weigh C. race D. protest
25. A. Furthermore B. Therefore C. Likewise D. However
26. A. sound B. result C. sight D. risk
27. A. pumped B. escaped C. stopped D. learned
28. A. same B. opposite C. great D. latter
29. A. computer B. lab C. team D. analysis
30. A. in charge B. under control C. in demand D. under pressure
31. A. preventing B. following C. deepening D. affecting
32. A. outweighed B. outdated C. outperformed D. outsourced
33. A. Preciously B. Surprisingly C. Unfortunately D. Arguably
34. A. crisis B. prime C. development D. mistake
35. A. according to B. along with C. apart from D. rather than

### 【你的答案】
21. D | 22. A | 23. C | 24. B | 25. A | 26. D | 27. C | 28. B | 29. B | 30. D | 31. B | 32. A | 33. A | 34. D | 35. D

### 【正确答案】
21. **C. cooperating** | 22. **B. pressure** | 23. **C. manageable** | 24. **B. weigh** | 25. **D. However** | 26. **D. risk** | 27. **C. stopped** | 28. **B. opposite** | 29. **B. lab** | 30. **A. in charge** | 31. **B. following** | 32. **C. outperformed** | 33. **C. Unfortunately** | 34. **A. crisis** | 35. **D. rather than** | **正确率：8/15**

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(21) 同义复现判断失误**：`finding common ground`（寻找共同点）是协作性行为，cooperating（合作）与之语义一致。deciding（做决定）太泛，与前文无逻辑关联。
2. **(22) 固定搭配/主题词遗漏**：`when the pressure is on` 是固定表达。全文主题是"压力下的决策差异"，后文反复出现 stress，上下文强烈呼应。
3. **(25) 逻辑关系判断错误**：正常情况下男女决策相似 → **然而**，加上压力后分道扬镳。这是**转折**关系，选 However。Furthermore（此外）表递进，逻辑错误。
4. **(30) 语义理解偏差**：`women in charge` = 掌权的女性/女性领导者，呼应首段 *we need more women*（在决策层）和后文 *positions of power*。under pressure 语义不对——文章讨论的是"谁掌权"而非"女性是否有压力"。
5. **(32) 近义词辨析不清**：outperformed = 业绩/表现优于；outweighed = 重量/重要性超过。此处比较公司业绩，应用 outperformed。
6. **(33) 词义不认识/态度词误判**：Unfortunately 表达遗憾和转折——女性有优势，但不幸的是只在危机时才被推上领导岗位。Preciously（珍重地）语义完全不通。
7. **(34) 上下文呼应遗漏**：`glass cliff` 概念——女性被安排在**危机**时刻领导。后文 GM 和 Yahoo 例子都是"被请来收拾烂摊子"。crisis 与全文 stress 主题呼应。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 固定搭配：when the pressure is on / weigh A against B / do the opposite / women in charge / rather than
- 同义复现：finding common ground → cooperating；positions of power → in charge
- 逻辑关系：转折（However）、递进（Furthermore）、因果（Therefore）
- 近义词辨析：outperformed（业绩优于）vs outweighed（重要性超过）
- 态度词：Unfortunately（遗憾）、Surprisingly（惊讶）、Arguably（可以说）
- 核心概念：glass cliff（玻璃悬崖）

### 【解题思路】
1. **把握全文主题**：本文主题是"压力下的性别决策差异"，看到 pressure/stress 就要注意同义替换
2. **同义复现**：前文的关键词往往在后文以同义词形式重现（common ground → cooperating）
3. **逻辑关系优先**：完形填空每道题都有逻辑线索，先判断是转折、递进、因果还是并列
4. **固定搭配**：看到 `weigh ___ against ___` 直接对应 weigh；`do the ___` 对应 opposite
5. **排除不认识的词**：选项中出现不认识的词（如 Preciously）要高度警惕，通常不是正确答案
6. **上下文呼应**：34 题 crisis 与全文 stress 主题呼应，30 题 in charge 与首尾段 positions of power 呼应

### 💡 举一反三

1. **weigh A against B**：We need to **weigh the costs against the benefits** before making a decision.
2. **when the pressure is on**：She performs best **when the pressure is on**.
3. **women in charge**：The company has **more women in charge** than any other in the industry.
4. **glass cliff**：The term **glass cliff** refers to the phenomenon where women and minorities are more likely to be appointed to leadership positions during times of crisis.
5. **rather than**：We should focus on solutions **rather than** dwelling on problems.

## 📝 错题 #3

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：选句填空（Week 9 - The Great Migration）

### 【题目原文】
**Directions:** Read the following passage. Fill in each blank with a proper sentence given in the box. Each sentence can be used only once. Note that there are two more sentences than you need.

**选项：**
A. That was against the law for African Americans at the time.
B. But during World War I, migration from Europe almost stopped.
C. The South actually did everything it could to keep the people from leaving.
D. The people of the Great Migration met with tremendous resistance in the North.
E. So this ended up being nearly a complete redistribution of part of an entire people.
F. They finally chose what they would do with their talents and where they would pursue them.

**The Great Migration**

The Great Migration began when the North had a labor problem. The North had been relying on cheap labor from Europe—immigrants from Europe—to work the factories, the foundries and the steel mills. **(36)___** As a result, the North decided to go and find the cheapest labor in the land, which meant many of African Americans in the South were not even being paid for their hard work. Many were sharecroppers working for the right to live on the land that they were farming.

But it turned out that the South did not take kindly to this poaching of its cheap labor. **(37)___** They would arrest people from the railroad platforms and from their train seats. And when there were too many people to arrest, they would wave the train on through so that people who had been hoping and saving and praying for the chance to get to freedom had to figure out: How now will we get out?

Before the Great Migration began, 90 percent of all African Americans were living in the South, where they were nearly held captive. But by the time this Great Migration was over, nearly half were living all over the rest of the country. **(38)___**

This Great Migration was the first time in American history that the lowest caste people signaled that they had options for themselves and were willing to take them. **(39)___** Think about those cotton fields, rice plantations, tobacco fields and sugar plantations, where there were opera singers, jazz musicians, playwrights, novelists, surgeons, attorneys, accountants, professors, and journalists. And how do we know that? We know that because that is what they and their children and now their grandchildren and even great-grandchildren have often chosen to become once they had the chance to choose for themselves what they would do with their God-given talents.

### 【你的答案】
36. B | 37. A | 38. C | 39. F

### 【正确答案】
36. **B** ✅ | 37. **C** ❌ | 38. **E** ❌ | 39. **F** ✅ | **正确率：2/4**

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(37) 总述+举例关系判断失误**：空格后是具体行动（逮捕火车站上的人、放行火车）。**C**（南方尽其所能阻止人们离开）是**总述**，后文的逮捕、放行火车都是**举例**支持 C。A（这对非裔美国人来说是违法的）语义不够贴切——这段强调的是南方**实际做了什么**来阻止，而非法律是否合法。
2. **(38) 数据总结句遗漏**：前文是数据对比——从"90% 在南方"到"近一半遍布全国"，这是一个**人口重新分布**的结果。**E**（这最终导致了整个民族一部分人的近乎完全重新分布）完美总结了这个数据变化。C（南方阻止人们离开）这段讲的是南方阻挠，但 38 题的位置是在讲迁徙**完成后的结果**，时间线不对。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 总述+举例：C 是总述，逮捕/放行火车是举例
- 数据总结：E 是对 90%→近一半的数据总结
- 语义呼应：F 的 chosen 与文末 chosen to become 形成语义链条
- 段落基调：选句要符合段落整体情感倾向（39 题 D 与段落积极基调不符）

### 【解题思路】
1. **找总述+举例**：空格后是具体例子 → 空格是总述句（37 题）
2. **找数据总结**：前文有具体数据变化 → 空格是总结句（38 题）
3. **注意段落基调**：选句要符合段落整体情感倾向，39 题段落是积极肯定，D 是消极阻力，基调不符
4. **关键词复现**：F 的 chosen 与文末 chosen to become 形成呼应
5. **排除干扰项**：A 的"违法"文中无法律依据线索；D 的"阻力"与段落基调不符

### 💡 举一反三

1. **总述+举例结构**：The government took many measures to control pollution. **First**, factories were required to install filters. **Second**, emissions were strictly limited.
2. **数据总结句**：In 1900, only 10% of Americans had access to electricity. By 1950, that figure had risen to 90%. **This represented a dramatic transformation of American life.**
3. **段落基调匹配**：选句填空要注意段落整体情感倾向，积极段落不选消极句，消极段落不选积极句。
