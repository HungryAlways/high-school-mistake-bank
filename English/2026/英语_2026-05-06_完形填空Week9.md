## 📝 错题 #2

**学科**：英语 | **章节**：完形填空（Week 9 - 女性在压力下的决策）

### 【题目原文】
**Directions:** For each blank in the following passage there are four words or phrases marked A, B, C and D. Fill in each blank with the word or phrase that best fits the context.

Recently, Senator Kirsten Gillibrand of New York said that if we want to fix the gridlock in Congress, we need more women. Women are more focused on finding common ground and **(21)___**, she argued. Besides, neuroscientists have uncovered evidence suggesting that, when the **(22)___** is on, women bring unique strengths to decision making.

Mara Mather and Nichole R. Lighthall, two cognitive neuroscientists, are two of the many researchers who have found that under normal circumstances, when everything is low-key and **(23)___**, men and women make decisions about risk in similar ways. We gather the best information we can, we **(24)___** potential costs against potential gains, and then we choose how to act. **(25)___**, add stress to the situation and men and women begin to part ways.

Dr. Mather and her team taught people a simple computer gambling game, in which they got points for inflating digital balloons. The more they inflated each balloon, the greater its value, and the **(26)___** of popping it. When they were relaxed, men and women took similar risks and averaged a similar number of pumps. But after experiencing the cold water, the stressed women **(27)___** sooner, cashing out their winnings and going with the more guaranteed win. Stressed men did just the **(28)___**. They kept pumping—in one study averaging about 50 percent more pumps than the women—and risking more.

Of course, just because it works this way in a(n) **(29)___** doesn't mean the same thing happens in the messy real world. Do organizations with women **(30)___** actually make less risky and more empathetic decisions in stressful circumstances?

Some evidence suggests they do. Credit Suisse examined almost 2,400 global corporations from 2005 to 2011—including the years directly preceding and **(31)___** the financial crisis—and found that companies with at least one woman on their boards **(32)___** comparable companies with all-male boards by 26 percent.

**(33)___**, what often happens is that women are asked to lead only during periods of intense stress. It's called the glass cliff, in which highly qualified women are asked to lead organizations only in times of **(34)___**. Think of Mary T. Barra at General Motors and Marissa Mayer at Yahoo, who were both brought in only after things had begun to fall apart. If more women were key decision makers, perhaps organizations could respond effectively to small stresses, **(35)___** letting them escalate into huge ones. There's a good reason that we'd benefit from more women in positions of power.

### 【错误原因分析】
1. **(21) 同义复现判断失误**：`finding common ground`（寻找共同点）是协作性行为，cooperating（合作）与之语义一致。deciding（做决定）太泛，与前文无逻辑关联。
2. **(22) 固定搭配/主题词遗漏**：`when the pressure is on` 是固定表达。全文主题是"压力下的决策差异"，后文反复出现 stress，上下文强烈呼应。
3. **(25) 逻辑关系判断错误**：正常情况下男女决策相似 → **然而**，加上压力后分道扬镳。这是**转折**关系，选 However。Furthermore（此外）表递进，逻辑错误。
4. **(30) 语义理解偏差**：`women in charge` = 掌权的女性/女性领导者，呼应首段 *we need more women*（在决策层）和后文 *positions of power*。under pressure 语义不对——文章讨论的是"谁掌权"而非"女性是否有压力"。
5. **(32) 近义词辨析不清**：outperformed = 业绩/表现优于；outweighed = 重量/重要性超过。此处比较公司业绩，应用 outperformed。
6. **(33) 词义不认识/态度词误判**：Unfortunately 表达遗憾和转折——女性有优势，但不幸的是只在危机时才被推上领导岗位。Preciously（珍重地）语义完全不通。
7. **(34) 上下文呼应遗漏**：`glass cliff` 概念——女性被安排在**危机**时刻领导。后文 GM 和 Yahoo 例子都是"被请来收拾烂摊子"。crisis 与全文 stress 主题呼应。

### 【涉及知识点】
- 固定搭配：when the pressure is on / weigh A against B / do the opposite / women in charge / rather than
- 同义复现：finding common ground → cooperating；positions of power → in charge
- 逻辑关系：转折（However）、递进（Furthermore）、因果（Therefore）
- 近义词辨析：outperformed（业绩优于）vs outweighed（重要性超过）
- 态度词：Unfortunately（遗憾）、Surprisingly（惊讶）、Arguably（可以说）
- 核心概念：glass cliff（玻璃悬崖）

### 【解题思路】
1. **把握全文主题**：本文主题是"压力下的性别决策差异"，看到 pressure/stress 就要注意同义替换
2. **同义复现**：前文的关键词往往在后文以同义词形式重现（common ground → cooperating）
3. **逻辑关系优先**：完形填空每道题都有逻辑线索，先判断是转折、递进、因果还是并列
4. **固定搭配**：看到 `weigh ___ against ___` 直接对应 weigh；`do the ___` 对应 opposite
5. **排除不认识的词**：选项中出现不认识的词（如 Preciously）要高度警惕，通常不是正确答案
6. **上下文呼应**：34 题 crisis 与全文 stress 主题呼应，30 题 in charge 与首尾段 positions of power 呼应

### 💡 举一反三

1. **weigh A against B**：We need to **weigh the costs against the benefits** before making a decision.
2. **when the pressure is on**：She performs best **when the pressure is on**.
3. **women in charge**：The company has **more women in charge** than any other in the industry.
4. **glass cliff**：The term **glass cliff** refers to the phenomenon where women and minorities are more likely to be appointed to leadership positions during times of crisis.
5. **rather than**：We should focus on solutions **rather than** dwelling on problems.
